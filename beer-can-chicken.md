# Beer Can Chicken

The steam rising from the beer keeps the meat beautifully juicy as it cooks. Use a beer can or one of the vertical roasters you can now buy, which are steadier to handle. Cider works just as well as beer. A great centrepiece for a barbecue party.

**Source:** Hairy Bikers  
**Tags:** BBQ, Roast, Summer

**Prep time:** 15 minutes  
**Cook time:** 1–1½ hours + 20 minutes resting  
**Serves:** 4–6

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Whole chicken, giblets removed | 1 × 1.8kg |
| Sweet smoked paprika | 1 tbsp |
| Mustard powder | 1 tbsp |
| Cayenne pepper | ½ tsp |
| Garlic powder | 1 tsp |
| Dried oregano | 1 tsp |
| Light soft brown sugar or honey | 1 tbsp |
| Lemon zest | zest of 1 lemon |
| Butter, softened | 50g |
| Can of beer (or cider) | 440ml |
| Sea salt and freshly ground black pepper | to taste |
| Lemon wedges | to serve |

---

## Method

1. Prepare your barbecue or preheat your oven to its highest setting.

2. Season the inside of the chicken with salt. Mix the paprika, mustard powder, cayenne, garlic powder, oregano, sugar or honey and lemon zest together in a small bowl with plenty of black pepper and 1 tsp salt. Add the butter and knead everything together, then rub the mixture all over the chicken, covering every bit of skin.

3. Pour out half the beer from the can. Sit the chicken over the open can and place it upright on a roasting tin.

4. **Oven:** Cook at the highest temperature for 15 minutes, then turn the oven down to 200°C / Fan 180°C / Gas 6 and roast for a further 45–60 minutes until cooked through.

5. **Barbecue:** Cook over very hot coals with the lid on for about 1 hour 20 minutes, checking the coals regularly, until cooked through.

6. Check the chicken is done — juices should run clear or a probe thermometer should read 72°C in the thickest part of the thigh. Cover with foil and rest for 20 minutes before serving.

7. Serve with lemon wedges — the juice really lifts the flavour.

---

## Notes

- A vertical roaster (purpose-built stand) is steadier than balancing on the can itself, and easier to handle when hot.
- Cider works beautifully as a beer substitute.
- Pour the half-can of beer into a glass — chef's perks.
