# High Protein Breakfast Bagels

10/10. No notes. Elite.

**Source:** Louis Howard PT (credit: @Aussie Fitness)  
**Tags:** Breakfast, High Protein, Eggs, Brunch  
**Equipment:** Hob, frying pan

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 15 minutes  
**Calories:** 513 per bagel  
**Macros per bagel:** 40g protein  
**Makes:** 8 (scale down freely — see notes)

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Bacon, sliced | 400g |
| Eggs | 16 |
| Parmesan, freshly grated | 10g |
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Onion & garlic powder | 1 tsp |
| Low fat cottage cheese, blended | 400g |
| Bagels, toasted | 8 |
| Cheddar cheese slices | 8 |
| Sriracha or hot sauce (optional) | 25g |

---

## Method

1. Cook the bacon in a frying pan until crispy. Set aside.

2. Crack the eggs into a large bowl. Add the blended cottage cheese, parmesan, salt and onion & garlic powder. Mix well.

3. Pour enough of the egg mix into a frying pan to cover the bottom. Put a lid on and cook for about 2 minutes until just set.

4. Fold into a pocket shape and set aside. Repeat for each bagel.

5. Toast the bagels. Add sriracha if using, then lay in an egg pocket, then the bacon, then a slice of cheddar. Put the lid back on the pan briefly to melt the cheese, or pop under the grill for a minute.

6. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Blending the cottage cheese before adding it is important — it incorporates smoothly into the egg mix and you won't taste it, but it boosts the protein significantly.
- Scale down easily — the egg mix keeps in the fridge for a day so you can make 2 bagels now and come back to the rest.
- The folded egg pocket is easier to manage if you use a pan roughly the same width as your bagel.
