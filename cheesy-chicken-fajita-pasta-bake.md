# Cheesy Chicken Fajita Pasta Bake

Solid 9/10. Would smash again. Easy, filling, high protein and actually tastes like real food.

**Source:** Louis Howard PT (credit: @Dave Fell)  
**Tags:** Chicken, Pasta, High Protein, Tray Bake, Main  
**Equipment:** Oven, large ovenproof dish

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 50 minutes  
**Calories:** 551 per serving  
**Macros per serving:** 57g protein · 55g carbs · 13g fat  
**Serves:** 4

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Pasta (dry weight) | 200g |
| Chicken breast, diced | 650g |
| Mozzarella, grated | 80g |
| Onion, diced | 1 |
| Peppers, diced | 2 |
| Low-fat cream cheese | 200g |
| Lazy garlic | 4 tsp |
| Fajita seasoning packet | 1 |
| Tinned chopped tomatoes | 400g |
| Water | 100ml |
| Salt and black pepper | to taste |

---

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 190°C / Fan 170°C.

2. Dice the chicken, onion and peppers.

3. Add everything except the mozzarella into a large ovenproof dish — pasta, chicken, veg, cream cheese, garlic, fajita seasoning, chopped tomatoes, salt and pepper.

4. Rinse the tomato tin out with the 100ml water and pour into the dish. Mix everything together really well so it's evenly coated.

5. Sprinkle the mozzarella evenly over the top.

6. Cover tightly with foil and bake for 45 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for a further 5 minutes to melt and colour the cheese.

7. Leave to rest for 5–10 minutes before serving — the sauce thickens and the pasta soaks everything up.

---

## Notes

- The tin-rinse step is important — gets every bit of sauce in and adds just enough liquid for the pasta to cook through.
- Resting after baking makes a noticeable difference to the texture — don't skip it.
- Old El Paso fajita mix works well here; any brand is fine.
- Very fat-loss friendly for how filling it is.
