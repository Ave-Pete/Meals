# Thai Pot-Roast Chicken

Si's son Alex invented this and rang him the moment he'd tasted it. It's fabulous and has become a firm family favourite.

**Source:** Hairy Bikers  
**Tags:** Main, Roast, Thai, Chillies  
**Video:** [Thai Pot-Roast Chicken – Hairy Bikers Family Favourites – YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=nYkm5HNnATE)

**Prep time:** 30 minutes  
**Cook time:** 2 hours  
**Serves:** 4–6

---

## Ingredients

### Chicken & Stuffing

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Whole chicken | 1 |
| Thai basil sprig | 1 |
| Coriander sprigs | a few |
| Lemongrass stalk, bruised | 1 |
| Lime leaves, bruised | 2 |
| Lime zest, pared | 1 piece |
| Garlic cloves, bruised | 3 |
| Salt | to taste |

### For the Pot

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Olive oil | 2 tbsp |
| Banana shallots, peeled | 8 |
| Thai red curry paste | 4 tbsp |
| Coconut milk | 200ml |
| Chicken stock | 200ml |
| Fish sauce | 1–2 tbsp |
| Lime leaves, shredded | 2 |
| Lime juice | juice of ½ lime |

### To Serve

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Coriander leaves, roughly torn | a handful |
| Thai basil, roughly torn | a handful |
| Spring onions, halved and shredded | 3 |
| Red chillies, finely sliced | 2 |
| Lime wedges | to serve |

---

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 200°C / Fan 180°C / Gas 6.

2. Season the inside and outside of the chicken with salt. Stuff the cavity with the herbs, lemongrass, lime leaves, lime zest and garlic.

3. Heat half the oil in a large flameproof casserole dish. Add the shallots and sear until lightly browned all over, then remove and set aside.

4. Add the remaining olive oil and the red curry paste to the dish. Stir over the heat for a few minutes until the paste has darkened slightly and the aroma has strengthened.

5. Add the chicken breast-side down and sear for a couple of minutes, then remove and set aside. Add the coconut milk, stock, 1 tbsp fish sauce, shredded lime leaves and lime juice to the dish. Deglaze the base well, then return the chicken breast-side up.

6. Cover the casserole and cook in the oven for about 40 minutes until the chicken is almost cooked through. Remove the lid, add the seared shallots and return to the oven for a further 20–25 minutes until the sauce has reduced slightly and both the shallots and chicken are tender. Check the chicken is cooked — a probe thermometer should read 72°C in the thickest part of the thigh, or the juices should run clear.

7. Remove from the oven, taste the sauce and add more fish sauce if needed. Rest for 10 minutes, then serve garnished with coriander, Thai basil, spring onions and chillies, with lime wedges on the side. Good with rice or noodles with fresh ginger stirred through.

---

## Notes

- Thai basil has a slightly aniseed flavour and is worth seeking out — most large supermarkets stock it. Regular basil is not a substitute.
- Banana shallots are larger and milder than round shallots — use 4–5 round shallots per banana shallot if needed.
