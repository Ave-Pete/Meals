# Pickled Onion Bhajis

Fabulous enough as regular bhajis, but with the extra flavour from lightly pickling the onion slices, these are something else. Make a plateful, pour yourself a cold beer and feel mega content.

**Source:** Hairy Bikers  
**Tags:** Vegetarian, Curry, Indian, Starter, Snack, Brunch

**Prep time:** 20 minutes + 1 hour pickling  
**Cook time:** 15 minutes  
**Serves:** 4

---

## Ingredients

### Pickled Onions

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Granulated sugar | 1 tsp |
| Salt | 2 tsp |
| Chilli flakes | 1 tsp |
| Cider or red wine vinegar | 50ml |
| Medium onions, finely sliced | 2 |

### Batter

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Gram (chickpea) flour | 70g |
| Rice flour | 30g |
| Baking powder | 1 tsp |
| Ground turmeric | 1 tsp |
| Ground cumin | 1 tsp |
| Ground cinnamon | ½ tsp |
| Root ginger, peeled and grated | 15g |
| Garlic cloves, crushed or grated | 3 |
| Green chillies, finely chopped | 2 |
| Coriander stems, finely chopped | 2 tbsp |
| Coconut oil, melted (or coconut yoghurt) | 1 tbsp |
| Lemon juice | 1 tbsp |

### To Fry

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Vegetable or groundnut oil | enough to half-fill the pan |

### Dipping Sauce

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Natural yoghurt | 200ml |
| Mango chutney | 50ml |
| Coriander leaves, finely chopped | a handful |

---

## Method

1. **Pickle the onions.** Mix the sugar, salt, chilli flakes and vinegar in a bowl and stir until dissolved. Add the onions, stir to coat and press them down into the vinegar. Top with just enough water to cover. Set aside for at least 1 hour, then drain thoroughly and dry on kitchen paper.

2. **Make the batter.** Whisk all the batter ingredients together, then add just enough water to reach the consistency of double cream. Stir in the drained pickled onions.

3. **Heat the oil.** Half-fill a large saucepan or deep-fat fryer with oil and heat to 180°C. Do not leave the pan unattended.

4. **Fry the bhajis.** Drop heaped tablespoons of mixture into the oil a few at a time — don't crowd the pan or the temperature will drop and they'll go soggy. Fry for 2–3 minutes each side until a deep golden brown. Drain on kitchen paper.

5. **Make the dipping sauce.** Stir the yoghurt, mango chutney and coriander together. Serve alongside the hot bhajis.

---

## Notes

- Gram flour (chickpea flour) is available in most supermarkets.
- If you can't find rice flour, blitz uncooked rice to a fine powder in a spice grinder or high-powered blender. It's the secret to a really crisp batter.
- To make vegan: use plant-based yoghurt in the batter and for the dipping sauce.
- Pickling for longer than an hour is fine and adds more depth.
