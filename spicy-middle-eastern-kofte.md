# Spicy Middle Eastern Kofte

A doddle to make and perfect for the barbecue. Serve in a pitta with tzatziki, or spear with cocktail sticks as a sharing starter.

**Source:** Hairy Bikers  
**Tags:** BBQ, Brunch, Lunch, Starter, Middle Eastern

**Prep time:** less than 30 minutes  
**Cook time:** 10–12 minutes  
**Makes:** 16  
**Equipment:** Oven or BBQ · Air fryer option below

---

## Ingredients

### Kofte

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Lamb mince | 400g |
| Shallot, very finely chopped | 1 |
| Garlic clove, crushed | 1 |
| Sumac | 1 tbsp |
| Garam masala | 2 tsp |
| Cracked black pepper | 1 tsp |
| Sea salt | 1 tsp |

### Glaze

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Clear honey | 4 tbsp |
| Pomegranate molasses | 4 tbsp |
| Pomegranate, seeds only | ½ |

---

## Method

1. Preheat the oven to 180°C / Fan 160°C / Gas 4, or light the barbecue.

2. Mix all the kofte ingredients together in a large bowl until well combined — use your hands for best results.

3. Divide the mixture into 16 equal portions and roll each into a small sausage shape. Place on a non-stick baking tray and cook for 10–12 minutes until cooked through.

4. Meanwhile, gently warm the honey and pomegranate molasses together in a small pan. Keep warm.

5. Remove the kofte from the oven, spear each one with a cocktail stick and arrange on a serving platter. Brush with the warm glaze and scatter over the pomegranate seeds. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Works equally well with turkey, beef, chicken or pork mince.
- Sumac and pomegranate molasses are available in most supermarkets (world food aisle) or online.
- On the barbecue, thread onto skewers instead of baking.

## Air Fryer

Preheat to 180°C. Place the kofte in the basket with a little space between each. Cook for 10–12 minutes, turning once halfway, until cooked through and nicely browned. No need to grease the basket — the meat has enough fat. Works brilliantly in the air fryer; arguably better than the oven as you get a better colour all round.
