# Prawn and Chorizo Bites with Garlic Oil

Simple, summery and seriously good. The garlic oil takes minutes to make and keeps all summer — make a big batch and use it on everything.

**Source:** Si King (Hairy Bikers)  
**Tags:** BBQ, Seafood, Summer, Mediterranean, Spanish

**Prep time:** less than 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 5–6 minutes  
**Serves:** 2–3  
**Equipment:** Grill or BBQ · Air fryer option below

---

## Ingredients

### Garlic Oil

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Garlic cloves, crushed | 8 |
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Black pepper | 1 tbsp |
| Good quality olive oil | 50cl (500ml) |

### Prawn and Chorizo Bites

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Tiger prawns | 500g |
| Cooking chorizo sausage | 1 (as spicy as you like) |
| Flat-leaf parsley | 1 bunch |
| Lime | 1 |
| Garlic oil | as needed |

---

## Method

1. **Make the garlic oil.** Add the garlic, salt, pepper and olive oil to a small pan. Gently warm the oil — do not boil. Allow to cool completely, then pour into a screw-top or kilner jar. Shake well. Keeps for several weeks in the fridge.

2. **Prepare the bites.** Slice the chorizo to the same thickness as the prawns. Fit a slice of chorizo into each prawn (tucked into the curve).

3. **Grill.** Lay the prawns on a grill and brush generously with garlic oil. BBQ for 3 minutes each side, brushing with more garlic oil while they cook.

4. **Finish.** Tip all the prawns and chorizo into a bowl. Add the zest of the lime, the juice of half the lime, the roughly chopped parsley and a good splash more garlic oil. Toss gently and serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Cooking chorizo (the soft, raw kind) is essential here — cured chorizo won't work the same way.
- The garlic oil recipe makes a large batch — it's meant to be made ahead and kept through summer. Scale down if you just want enough for this dish.
- Also excellent drizzled over bread, grilled vegetables or fish.

## Air Fryer

Preheat to 180°C. Brush the prepared prawns with garlic oil and place in the basket in a single layer. Cook for 6–8 minutes, turning once halfway, until the prawns are pink and the chorizo has crisped at the edges. Finish in the bowl with lime, parsley and more garlic oil as normal. Works well — you lose the slight char of the BBQ but the flavour is still excellent.
