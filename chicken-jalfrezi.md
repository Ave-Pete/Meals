# Chicken Jalfrezi

A culinary triumph for curry-loving dieters – what's not to like? A classic reworked to be lighter without losing any of the flavour.

**Source:** Hairy Dieters  
**Tags:** Curry, Main, Hairy Dieters

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 15 minutes  
**Calories:** 279 per serving

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | 2 servings | 4 servings (base) | 6 servings | 8 servings |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Long green chillies | 3 | 6 | 9 | 12 |
| Boneless, skinless chicken breasts | 2 | 4 | 6 | 8 |
| Sunflower oil | 1 tbsp | 2 tbsp | 3 tbsp | 4 tbsp |
| Garlic cloves, finely chopped | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| Ripe tomatoes, chopped | 1½ | 3 | 4½ | 6 |
| Ground cumin | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1½ tbsp | 2 tbsp |
| Garam masala | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1½ tbsp | 2 tbsp |
| Ground turmeric | ½ tsp | 1 tsp | 1½ tsp | 2 tsp |
| Caster sugar | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1½ tbsp | 2 tbsp |
| Flaked sea salt | ½ tsp | 1 tsp | 1½ tsp | 2 tsp |
| Cold water | 100ml | 200ml | 300ml | 400ml |
| Low-fat natural yoghurt | 1 tbsp | 2 tbsp | 3 tbsp | 4 tbsp |
| Medium onion, cut into wedges | ½ | 1 | 1½ | 2 |
| Green pepper, cut into 3cm chunks | ½ | 1 | 1½ | 2 |
| Tomatoes, quartered | 1 | 2 | 3 | 4 |
| Cornflour | 1 tsp | 2 tsp | 3 tsp | 4 tsp |
| Water (for cornflour) | ½ tbsp | 1 tbsp | 1½ tbsp | 2 tbsp |

---

## Method

1. Finely chop 4 of the chillies — deseed a couple or all of them if you prefer less heat. Split the remaining 2 chillies from stalk to tip on one side, leaving them whole with seeds intact. Cut each chicken breast into 7–8 bite-sized chunks.

2. Heat 1 tbsp of the oil in a large, deep non-stick frying pan or wok over a high heat. Add the garlic, chopped chillies, chopped tomatoes, cumin, garam masala, turmeric, sugar and salt. Stir-fry for 3–4 minutes until the vegetables soften. Do not let the garlic or spices burn or they will turn bitter.

3. Add the chicken pieces and whole chillies and cook for 3 minutes, turning the chicken regularly. Pour over the water, stir in the yoghurt and reduce the heat slightly — the sauce should simmer. Cook for about 8 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has reduced by about a third. The yoghurt may separate initially but will incorporate into the sauce.

4. While the chicken cooks, heat the remaining oil in a separate pan and stir-fry the onion and pepper over a high heat for 3–4 minutes until lightly browned. Add the quartered tomatoes and fry for a further 2–3 minutes until just tender.

5. Mix the cornflour with the water to form a smooth paste. Stir into the chicken sauce and simmer for a few seconds until thickened, stirring constantly.

6. Remove from the heat, add the stir-fried vegetables and toss together lightly. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- Don't eat the whole chillies — they are for flavour only!
- The yoghurt will look like it's splitting at first — this is normal and it will disappear into the sauce.
