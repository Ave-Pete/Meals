# Chicken Tikka Masala

A long ingredient list, but well worth making your own masala paste instead of using a jar — you'll be rewarded with a truly tasty, fragrant dish. You'll need metal skewers for the chicken.

**Source:** Hairy Bikers  
**Tags:** Curry, Main, Indian  
**Video:** [Hairy Bikers Chicken Tikka Masala – YouTube](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=pBGnvr9OM-A)

**Prep time:** 30 minutes + 4 hours marinating (ideally overnight)  
**Cook time:** 30 minutes  
**Serves:** 4

---

## Ingredients

### Spice Mix & Marinade

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Cumin seeds | 2 tbsp |
| Coriander seeds | 2 tbsp |
| Whole cloves | 2 |
| Black peppercorns | 1 tsp |
| Cinnamon stick | small piece |
| Ground fenugreek | ½ tsp |
| Ground turmeric | 1½ tsp |
| Ground paprika | 2 tsp |
| Hot chilli powder | ½–1 tsp |
| Garlic cloves, crushed | 2 |
| Root ginger, peeled and finely grated | 20g |
| Natural yoghurt | 4 tbsp |
| Salt | 1 tsp |

### Chicken

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Boneless, skinless chicken breasts | 4 |
| Oil, for greasing skewers | a little |

### Masala Sauce

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Ghee, or butter + sunflower oil (see notes) | 4 tbsp ghee, or 2 tbsp butter + 2 tbsp sunflower oil |
| Medium onions, chopped | 3 |
| Garlic cloves, crushed | 4 |
| Root ginger, peeled and finely grated | 25g |
| Ground turmeric | ½ tsp |
| Tomato purée | 3 tbsp |
| Caster sugar | 2 tsp |
| Double cream | 2 tbsp |
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Water | 400ml |

### To Serve

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Rice | as needed |

---

## Method

1. **Make the spice mix.** Toast the cumin seeds, coriander seeds, cloves, peppercorns and cinnamon stick in a dry frying pan for 1–2 minutes, stirring, until lightly toasted. Tip into a mortar or spice grinder, add the fenugreek, turmeric, paprika, chilli powder and 1 tsp salt. Grind to a fine powder.

2. **Marinate the chicken.** Spoon 3 tbsp of the spice mix into a bowl and stir in the garlic, ginger and yoghurt. Cut each chicken breast into 7–8 pieces and stir into the spiced yoghurt. Cover and refrigerate for at least 4 hours, ideally overnight.

3. **Make the masala sauce.** Heat the ghee (or butter and oil) in a large non-stick saucepan. Add the onions, garlic and ginger, cover and cook over a low heat for 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. Remove the lid, increase the heat slightly and stir in the remaining spice mix plus the ½ tsp turmeric. Fry for 3 minutes, stirring regularly.

4. Stir in the tomato purée, caster sugar and 1 tsp salt. Fry for 2–3 minutes, stirring constantly. Add 400ml water, bring to a gentle simmer and cook for 5 minutes, adding the cream in the last 30 seconds. Remove from the heat and blitz with a stick blender until as smooth as possible. Pour into a bowl, cover and refrigerate until needed.

5. **Grill the chicken.** Thread the marinated chicken onto lightly greased metal skewers (about 6 chunks per skewer, leaving a gap between each piece). Preheat the grill to its hottest setting. Place on a rack over a foil-lined grill pan and grill as close to the heat as possible for 5 minutes. Turn and grill for a further 4–5 minutes until cooked through and lightly charred.

6. **Finish the sauce.** While the chicken grills, tip the masala sauce into a large frying pan and bring to a gentle simmer for 2–3 minutes. Slide the chicken off the skewers into the sauce using a fork. Stir well, bubble for a few seconds and serve hot with rice.

---

## Notes

- Reserve the remaining spice mix (you'll use 3 tbsp for the marinade, the rest goes into the sauce).
- Ghee gives the most authentic flavour; butter + sunflower oil is a good alternative.
- Marinating overnight makes a significant difference to the depth of flavour.
- The sauce can be made a day ahead and kept in the fridge.
