# 600 Calorie Big Stack Burger

A double-stacked smash burger that comes in at around 600 calories. Solid 9.5/10.

**Source:** [Louis Howard PT](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/276WBpe1sjI) (credit: @mealswithmax)  
**Tags:** Beef, Main, Fakeaway, High Protein  
**Equipment:** Hob, frying pan

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 10 minutes  
**Calories:** ~600  
**Serves:** 1

---

## Ingredients

### Burger

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| 5% lean beef mince | 160g |
| Burger bun | 1 (70g) |
| Cheddar cheese slice | 20g |
| White onion, diced | 20g |
| Crispy onions | 5g |
| Lettuce | a few leaves |
| Salt and black pepper | to taste |

### Sauce

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Ketchup | 10g |
| Light mayo | 10g |
| Dijon mustard | 5g |
| Pickle juice | 5ml |
| White wine vinegar | 5ml |
| Honey | 5g |
| Garlic granules | ¼ tsp |
| Onion powder | ¼ tsp |
| Paprika | ¼ tsp |
| Salt and black pepper | pinch |

---

## Method

1. Mix all the sauce ingredients together and set aside.

2. Divide the beef mince into two 80g portions and flatten into thin patties. Season both sides with salt and pepper.

3. Heat a frying pan until very hot. Cook the patties for 2–3 minutes per side until well browned. Lay the cheese over one patty in the last minute so it melts.

4. Toast the bun in the same pan.

5. Spread the sauce on both sides of the bun. Stack the patties, then top with lettuce, diced onion and crispy onions. Eat immediately.

---

## Notes

- 5% lean mince keeps the calories down without sacrificing too much flavour — don't go leaner or the patties will be dry.
- The pan needs to be very hot before the patties go in to get a good crust.
- Pickle juice is the secret in the sauce — use the liquid from a jar of gherkins.
