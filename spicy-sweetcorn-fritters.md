# Spicy Sweetcorn Fritters

Crispy little fritters with a kick — great as a starter with the dipping sauce, or served alongside salad or slices of ham.

**Source:** Hairy Dieters: Fast Food  
**Tags:** Vegetarian, BBQ

**Prep time:** 10 minutes  
**Cook time:** 15 minutes  
**Calories:** 57 per fritter  
**Makes:** 12 fritters

---

## Ingredients

> Scales are per batch (12 fritters). Scale up for more batches.

| Ingredient | 1 batch (12) | 2 batches (24) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Frozen sweetcorn | 300g | 600g |
| Eggs | 3 | 6 |
| Soy sauce | 1 tbsp | 2 tbsp |
| Sriracha (or similar hot sauce) | 1 tsp | 2 tsp |
| Plain flour | 60g | 120g |
| Coriander stems, finely chopped | 1 tbsp | 2 tbsp |
| Garlic clove, finely chopped | 1 | 2 |
| Spring onion, finely chopped | 1 | 2 |
| Low-cal oil spray | as needed | as needed |
| Flaked sea salt & black pepper | to taste | to taste |

### Dipping Sauce

| Ingredient | 1 batch | 2 batches |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Soy sauce | 2 tbsp | 4 tbsp |
| Lime juice | juice of 1 lime | juice of 2 limes |
| Sugar or honey | ½ tsp | 1 tsp |
| Red chilli flakes | 1 tsp | 2 tsp |
| Garlic clove, finely chopped | 1 | 2 |

---

## Method

1. Bring a kettle to the boil. Put the sweetcorn in a heatproof bowl, pour over boiling water and leave for a minute to defrost. Drain and set aside.

2. Beat the eggs in a bowl until well broken up. Add the soy sauce and hot sauce, season with salt and pepper. Sprinkle over the flour and whisk until smooth with no lumps. Stir in the coriander stems, garlic, spring onion and sweetcorn.

3. Heat a large frying pan and spray evenly with oil. Dollop heaped tablespoons of batter into the pan — cook 4–5 at a time, don't crowd the pan. Cook for 2–3 minutes until well browned underneath, then flip and cook the other side. Keep warm while you cook the remaining batches.

4. For the dipping sauce, stir the soy sauce, lime juice and sugar/honey together until the sugar dissolves. Add the chilli flakes and garlic and leave to stand until ready to serve.

---

## Notes

- Don't crowd the pan — it makes flipping much harder.
- Coriander stems have more flavour than the leaves and hold up better in the batter.
