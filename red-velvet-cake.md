# Red Velvet Cake

A stunning classic that's well worth the effort. Pete's first attempt using the Hummingbird Bakery recipe was, by all accounts, the nicest Red Velvet Cake he'd ever had!

**Source:** The Hummingbird Bakery Cookbook (cupcake section, scaled up for a cake)  
**Tags:** Cake, Bake

**Prep time:** ~30 minutes  
**Cook time:** 20–25 minutes (cake) / 15–18 minutes (cupcakes)  
**Calories:** ~550 per serving (estimate)

> **Note on scaling:** This recipe is given as a single cake serving ~10. Cakes don't scale well, so the quantities below are fixed. If you want a smaller batch, make cupcakes instead — the original Hummingbird recipe is in the cupcake section and the amounts below will make approximately 12 cupcakes.

---

## Ingredients

### Cake (serves ~10) / Cupcakes (makes ~12)

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Unsalted butter (room temp) | 120g |
| Caster sugar | 300g |
| Eggs | 2 |
| Cocoa powder | 20g |
| Red food colouring | 4 tbsp |
| Vanilla extract / paste | 1 tsp |
| Buttermilk | 240ml |
| Plain flour | 300g |
| Salt | 1 tsp |
| Bicarbonate of soda | 1 tsp |
| White wine vinegar | 3 tsp |

### Cream Cheese Frosting

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Icing sugar, sifted | 600g |
| Unsalted butter (room temp) | 100g |
| Cream cheese, cold | 250g |

---

## Method

1. Preheat oven to 170°C / 325°F (150°C fan). The recipe suggests three 20cm tins, but two 22cm tins work equally well.

2. Cream together the butter and sugar until pale and fluffy, then gradually incorporate the eggs.

3. Separately, combine the cocoa powder, red food colouring and vanilla to form a paste, then mix into the egg mixture.

4. Gradually add the buttermilk and flour, a little of each at a time, until fully combined.

5. Add the salt, bicarbonate of soda and white wine vinegar and mix until well combined.

6. Divide the batter between the cake tins and bake for 20–25 minutes.

7. **Frosting:** Whisk the butter first to loosen it, then gradually add the sifted icing sugar. Add the cold cream cheese at the end — do not overbeat or it will go runny.

8. Once the cakes are fully cooled, sandwich the layers with cream cheese frosting and top with the remaining frosting.

---

## Notes

- **Food colouring:** 40g of Dr Oetker Gel Food Colouring in Bright Red works well as a substitute for 4 tbsp liquid colouring. Avoid any "natural" colours and ensure it is bake-stable — natural colours will turn brown when cooked.
- **Vanilla:** Vanilla bean paste works just as well as vanilla extract.
- **Tins:** If you only have two 20cm tins, two 22cm tins are a good alternative.
- **Frosting tip:** Whisk the butter before adding the icing sugar to avoid clouds of icing sugar everywhere!
