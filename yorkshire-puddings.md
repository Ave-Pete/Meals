# Yorkshire Puddings

The classic. The keys are equal volumes of egg, milk and flour, a very hot oven, and smoking-hot fat before the batter goes in. Don't open the oven door.

**Source:** Traditional  
**Tags:** Bake, British, Sides, Roast  
**Equipment:** Oven, 12-hole muffin tin or Yorkshire pudding tin

**Prep time:** 5 minutes + 30 minutes resting  
**Cook time:** 20–25 minutes  
**Makes:** 12

---

## Ingredients

| Ingredient | Quantity |
| --- | --- |
| Eggs | 3 large |
| Whole milk | 140ml |
| Plain flour | 140ml (measured by volume, not weight) |
| Salt | a good pinch |
| Lard, dripping, or sunflower oil | enough to cover the base of each hole |

---

## Method

1. Crack the eggs into a jug and beat well. Add the milk and beat together. Sift in the flour and salt and whisk until completely smooth with no lumps. Leave to rest for at least 30 minutes at room temperature — don't skip this.

2. Preheat the oven to 230°C / Fan 210°C / Gas 8. Add a small amount of fat to each hole of the tin and place in the oven for 10–15 minutes until the fat is smoking hot.

3. Working quickly, pour the batter into the holes — they should sizzle immediately. Fill each hole about halfway. Shut the oven door and do not open it for at least 20 minutes.

4. Cook for 20–25 minutes until well risen, deep golden brown, and crisp. Serve immediately.

---

## Notes

- The equal-volumes rule (egg : milk : flour by volume) scales perfectly — use a measuring jug rather than weighing the flour.
- The fat must be smoking before the batter goes in. If it isn't, they won't rise.
- Do not open the oven door — the drop in temperature will collapse them.
- Lard or beef dripping gives the best flavour; sunflower oil works fine.
- Leftover batter keeps in the fridge overnight — give it a quick whisk before using.

## Air Fryer

Yorkshire puddings can be done in the air fryer using a silicone muffin tray or small ramekins. Preheat to 200°C for 5 minutes. Add a little oil to each cup and heat for 2–3 minutes until very hot. Pour in the batter quickly and cook for 14–16 minutes without opening the drawer. They rise well and get a good colour, though slightly less dramatic than the oven version. Useful when the oven is already taken up with a roast.
